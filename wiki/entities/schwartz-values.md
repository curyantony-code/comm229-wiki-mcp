# Schwartz's Value Theory

## What It Is
A theory of human values (Parks-Leduc et al., 2015, citing Schwartz) organizing 10 motivational value types in a circumplex where adjacent values are compatible and opposing values are in tension.

## The 10 Values
| Value | Description |
|---|---|
| **Power** | Social power, wealth, authority — being in charge of people and resources |
| **Achievement** | Socially recognized success — ambition, competence |
| **Hedonism** | Sensual pleasure — fun, enjoying life |
| **Stimulation** | Exciting, novel, challenging experiences — daring |
| **Self-direction** | Independence of thought and action — creativity, freedom, curiosity |
| **Universalism** | Promoting welfare of all people and nature — equality, social justice |
| **Benevolence** | Promoting welfare of close others — helpfulness, loyalty, honesty |
| **Conformity** | Controlling impulses to fulfill others' expectations — self-discipline, obedience |
| **Tradition** | Maintaining traditions — moderation, respect, devotion |
| **Security** | Safety and security of self, family, and nation — social order, cleanliness |

## Value Tensions (Opposing Values)
- **Stimulation vs. Security** — craving novelty vs. craving stability
- **Power vs. Universalism** — personal dominance vs. collective welfare
- **Hedonism vs. Conformity** — self-indulgence vs. self-discipline
- **Self-direction vs. Conformity/Tradition** — independence vs. adherence

## Applications in COMM 229

### Selection (wk5)
- **PO Fit (Person-Organization):** Value congruence between employee and organization
- Used to assess cultural fit; but must avoid cultural cloning (see [[concepts/dei]])

### Motivation (wk9)
- Values inform *valence* in Expectancy Theory — what outcomes an employee finds desirable
- SDT: security-oriented people may respond to job stability; achievement-oriented to skill development

### Management & Leadership (wk8)
- Case activity: "How would someone who prioritizes Security/Benevolence/Achievement/Universalism approach an AI-driven role reduction?" — shows value tensions in organizational decisions

## Common Misconceptions
- Values are relatively stable but not completely fixed
- High cultural fit (shared values) is not always better — diversity of values drives innovation (tension with DEI goals)
- The circumplex structure means *every* person holds all 10 values to varying degrees
