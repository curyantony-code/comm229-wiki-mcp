# Expectancy Theory (Vroom)

## What It Is
A process theory of motivation proposing that people choose to exert effort based on three perceived probabilities about effort → performance → outcomes. All three components multiply together — if any is zero, motivation collapses.

## Formula
**Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence**

## Components

| Component | Definition | Manager's Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Expectancy (E)** | Perceived probability that effort will lead to desired performance | "Can I perform well if I try?" |
| **Instrumentality (I)** | Perceived probability that performance will lead to desired outcomes | "If I perform, will I get the outcomes?" |
| **Valence (V)** | Perceived value (attractiveness) of the outcomes | "Are those outcomes worth it to me?" |

## Manager's Toolkit

| Problem | Root Cause | Solution |
|---|---|---|
| "I can't do it even if I try" | Low Expectancy | Training, resources, coaching, build self-efficacy |
| "Performing well won't get me anything" | Low Instrumentality | Clarify and deliver reward links; be trustworthy |
| "I don't care about the rewards" | Low Valence | Individualize rewards; find what the person values |

## Exam-Tested Scenario
> "I know that if I put in effort, I can perform well. But even if I perform well, I probably won't get the promotion."
→ **Low Instrumentality** (performance ↛ valued outcome)

## Applications in COMM 229
- **Performance Management (wk8):** Expectancy Theory explains why clear performance-outcome links (instrumentality) must be communicated; coaching builds expectancy
- **Compensation (wk9):** Compensation design must address valence (are the rewards desired?) and instrumentality (is the link between performance and pay clear?)
- **Goal Setting:** Goals that are achievable but challenging maintain expectancy; unrealistic goals crush it

## Connection to Other Theories
- **SDT:** Expectancy addresses the "can I?" (competence); SDT addresses the "do I want to?" (autonomous vs. controlled)
- **Equity Theory:** When instrumentality is perceived as unfair (others get more for the same performance), equity concerns emerge

## Common Misconceptions
- Expectancy theory does NOT say people are purely rational — perceived probabilities are subjective
- High valence alone doesn't motivate if expectancy or instrumentality is zero
- Instrumentality is about *trust* in the system, not just existence of rewards
