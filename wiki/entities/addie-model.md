# ADDIE Model (Instructional Systems Design)

## What it is

The ADDIE model is a 5-phase **Instructional Systems Design (ISD)** framework used to plan, deliver, and evaluate training programs. It ensures training is systematically designed to meet identified organizational needs and contribute to org goals. In COMM 229 (Schwind Ch12), it is the primary framework for the training design process.

## Components / Steps

### Phase 1 — Assess Needs
Identify what training is needed and whether training is the right solution.
- **Levels of analysis:**
  - *Organizational level:* Examine environment and strategy to determine training emphasis
  - *Task level:* Review job activities to identify required competencies
  - *Person level:* Identify which employees need training
- **4 guiding questions:**
  1. Is this issue important to organizational success?
  2. What KSAs (Knowledge, Skills, Abilities) do employees *need*?
  3. What KSAs do employees currently *have*?
  4. What is the gap between needed and actual?

### Phase 2 — Design
Plan how training will be structured and delivered.
- Define **instructional goals** (desired outcomes) and **trainee characteristics**
- Choose **training methods** aligned to desired learning outcomes
- Apply **learning principles:** clear outcomes, relevance, activity, problem-solving focus, feedback
- Develop assessment tools to measure learning

### Phase 3 — Develop
Create or gather training materials.
- Produce presentations, manuals, videos, e-learning modules
- Pilot test for clarity and effectiveness
- Review by subject matter experts (SMEs)

### Phase 4 — Implement (Deliver)
Deliver training to employees.
- Activate **motivation** (explain reasons and usefulness)
- Use **observation** (PPTs, demos, videos)
- Provide **positive reinforcement** (builds self-efficacy)
- Build on **experience** (adults are not blank slates)
- Use **spaced practice** (spread learning to allow digestion and integration)
- Encourage **metacognition & self-regulation** (reflection on what worked/didn't)

### Phase 5 — Evaluate
Measure effectiveness using **Kirkpatrick's Four Levels** (see [[entities/kirkpatrick-model]]):
1. **Reaction** — participant satisfaction
2. **Learning** — knowledge/skills gained
3. **Behaviour** — transfer of training (applying skills on the job)
4. **Results** — ROI / organizational impact

## Common Misconceptions

- **ADDIE ≠ a training method** — it is a *design and delivery framework*. Training methods (OJT, simulation, coaching) are chosen *within* Phase 2.
- **Evaluation ≠ Phase 5 only** — good ISD practice evaluates at each phase, but formal evaluation (Kirkpatrick) is at Phase 5.
- **Transfer of training** (applying skills on the job) = Kirkpatrick Level 3, not Level 2 (learning = knowledge acquired in the room).
