# Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle

## What It Is
A 4-stage model of experiential learning (Kolb, 1984) proposing that learning is a cyclical process requiring concrete experience, reflection, conceptualization, and experimentation.

## The 4 Stages

| Stage | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Concrete Experience** | Participate in a new situation or experience |
| **2** | **Reflective Observation** | Reflect upon the experience; compare with previous experiences; find inconsistencies |
| **3** | **Abstract Conceptualization** | Generate new ideas or modify understanding based on reflection |
| **4** | **Active Experimentation** | Test the new ideas and see what happens |

Then cycles back to Stage 1 with the new understanding.

## Applications in COMM 229
- **Training (wk7):** Drives the principle that training should include experience, reflection, and practice — not just lecture
- **Implementing training (wk7):** "We learn from experience, as long as we pause to reflect" — the reflection step (Stage 2) is often skipped in busy workplaces
- **Career development:** Explains why job rotation, secondments, and stretch assignments are powerful development tools

## Connection to Bandura (Social Learning Theory)
- Bandura: We learn by *observing* others (social/vicarious experience)
- Kolb: We learn by *doing* and *reflecting* (experiential)
- Both emphasize active, participatory learning over passive reception

## Common Misconceptions
- Kolb's cycle doesn't require starting at Stage 1 — you can enter at any stage
- The model implies *all 4 stages* are necessary for full learning; skipping reflection (Stage 2) is the most common gap in practice
