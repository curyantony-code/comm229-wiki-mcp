# Big 5 Personality Traits (OCEAN)

## What It Is
A widely validated model of personality structure (McCrae & Costa, 2003) describing five broad, relatively stable patterns of behaviour, thoughts, and emotions. Used in employee selection and development.

**Acronym: OCEAN**

## The Five Traits

| Trait | High Description | Low Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Openness to Experience** | Imaginative, curious, creative, original, insightful, intellectual, artistic | Conventional, practical, narrow-minded, rigid, uncreative |
| **Conscientiousness** | Organized, industrious, responsible, reliable, disciplined, hardworking | Careless, disorganized, negligent, unreliable |
| **Extraversion** | Sociable, outgoing, energetic, talkative, assertive, enthusiastic, dominant | Introverted, reserved, quiet, shy, withdrawn, passive |
| **Agreeableness** | Compassionate, polite, cooperative, trusting, kind, sympathetic, warm | Competitive, uncooperative, harsh, confrontational |
| **Neuroticism** | Anxious, insecure, self-conscious, emotionally unstable, sensitive | Emotionally stable, calm, resilient, relaxed, secure |

## Applications in COMM 229

### Selection (wk5)
- Conscientiousness is the **most consistent predictor of job performance** across occupations
- Extraversion predicts success in sales and management roles
- Agreeableness predicts performance in team-based work
- Neuroticism (low emotional stability) can predict turnover and absenteeism

### Diversity & Teams
- Big 5 traits affect interpersonal interactions, conflict styles, and team dynamics

## Common Misconceptions
- "High" is not always better — e.g., very high Agreeableness can predict poor negotiation; very high Conscientiousness can lead to rigidity
- Big 5 tests must be job-related to be used in selection (validity requirement)
- Neuroticism is the *reverse* end of emotional stability — high neuroticism = emotionally unstable
