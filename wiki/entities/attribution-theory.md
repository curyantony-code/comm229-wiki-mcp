# Attribution Theory

## What It Is
The process by which we assign causes to explain people's behaviour. We attribute behaviour either to dispositional (internal) factors or situational (external) factors.

## Attribution Cues

| Cue | Question | High → | Low → |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Consistency** | How often does the person do this in this situation? | Internal | External |
| **Consensus** | How many others do this in this situation? | External | Internal |
| **Distinctiveness** | Does the person do this in many situations? | External (low distinct. = internal) | Internal |

### Attribution Pattern
**Internal attribution (dispositional):**
- High consistency + Low consensus + Low distinctiveness

**External attribution (situational):**
- Low consistency + High consensus + High distinctiveness

## Attribution Biases

### Fundamental Attribution Error (FAE)
The tendency to **overemphasize internal (dispositional) causes** of *other people's* actions and underestimate situational factors.

> Example: Employee's performance declined after reassignment to unfamiliar software → supervisor attributes it to "not trying hard enough" instead of the situational challenge.

### Self-Serving Bias
The tendency to **attribute one's own successes to internal factors** and one's own **failures to external factors**.

> Example: Student gets a good grade → "I'm smart and studied hard." Student fails → "The exam was impossible."

## Application in COMM 229

### Performance Management (wk8)
- Supervisors committing FAE over-blame employees for performance gaps and miss organizational/job causes → wrong intervention
- Self-serving bias in employees can block acknowledgment of performance problems
- Rater training should address attribution biases

### Perception Biases in Appraisals
Related biases (not attribution per se, but similar mechanism):
- Halo effect, primacy/recency, central tendency, contrast errors — all involve systematic distortion of perception

## Common Misconceptions
- FAE is about judgments of *others*, not yourself (self-serving bias is the mirror for self-judgments)
- Attribution is not about who is *actually* responsible — it's about *perceived* cause
