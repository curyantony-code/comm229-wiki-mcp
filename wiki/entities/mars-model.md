# MARS Model

## What it is

The MARS model (McShane & Von Glinow) explains how four factors — **Motivation**, **Ability**, **Role Perceptions**, and **Situational Factors** — jointly determine individual behaviour and results. All four must be adequate; a deficiency in any one degrades performance regardless of the others.

## Components

| Factor | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Motivation** | Desire, drive, and willingness to perform a task (direction + intensity + persistence) |
| **Ability** | Natural aptitudes, learned capabilities, and habits required to successfully complete a task |
| **Role Perceptions** | Degree to which a person understands the job duties assigned to or expected of them |
| **Situational Factors** | Environmental conditions that facilitate or constrain behaviour (context, resources, time) |

## Behaviour & Results (Outputs)

The five categories of behaviour/results that MARS influences:

1. **Task performance** — voluntary, goal-directed behaviours contributing to org objectives
   - *Proficient:* How well employees perform efficiently and accurately
   - *Adaptive:* How well employees modify thoughts/behaviour for new/changing work settings
   - *Proactive:* How well employees anticipate and introduce new work patterns that benefit the org
2. **Organizational Citizenship Behaviours (OCBs)** — cooperation and helpfulness supporting the org's social/psychological context
3. **Counterproductive Work Behaviours (CWBs)** — voluntary behaviours that directly or indirectly harm the org
4. **Joining and staying with the organization** — commitment and turnover decisions
5. **Maintaining work attendance** — absenteeism

**Individual characteristics** feeding into the MARS factors: personality, values, self-concept, perceptions, emotions and attitudes, stress.

## Applications in COMM 229

- **Week 1 (Intro to OB):** MARS introduced as the foundational model for understanding individual behaviour
- **Wize Midterm 1 (Ch1):** Exam-tested model — each component frequently appears in MCQ scenario questions
- **Performance management (wk8):** MARS explains performance gaps (ability gap vs. motivation gap vs. role clarity gap)
- **Job design (wk2/Ch2):** Role perceptions improved through clear job descriptions; situational factors improved through job design

## Common Misconceptions

- **Ability ≠ motivation.** A highly motivated employee with low ability will still underperform. Diagnose which MARS factor is deficient before designing an intervention.
- **"Role perceptions"** is not about personality — it is specifically about understanding what the job requires.
- **Situational factors** are sometimes forgotten. Even a motivated, able employee who understands their role may fail due to lack of resources or time.
- **Adaptive task performance** ≠ Proactive task performance. Adaptive = modifying behaviour for *existing* change; Proactive = *initiating* new patterns.
