# Kirkpatrick's 4-Level Training Evaluation Model

## What It Is
A framework for evaluating the effectiveness of training programs across four levels of increasing rigour and organizational impact.

## The 4 Levels

| Level | Name | Question | Method |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Reaction** | Did they like it? Were they satisfied? | Post-training survey; "happy sheets" |
| **2** | **Learning** | Did they acquire new knowledge/skills? | Pre/post tests; skill demonstrations |
| **3** | **Behaviour** | Did they apply it on the job? | Observation; supervisor ratings; 360° |
| **4** | **Results** | Did it improve business KPIs? | ROI calculation; productivity metrics |

## Applications in COMM 229
- Wk7: Core evaluation framework for T&D programs
- Level 3 (Behaviour/Transfer) is the most commonly cited exam question: "employees not applying new skills after training" = Level 3 problem
- Level 4 = ROI (cost of training vs. benefits from improved quality, productivity, reduced waste)

## Common Misconceptions
- Level 1 (satisfaction/reaction) is the most common evaluation method but the *weakest* predictor of success
- Satisfaction does not guarantee learning; learning does not guarantee transfer
- Many organizations stop at Level 1 or 2 and miss the most important question (Level 3: did behaviour change?)

## Why Transfer Fails (Level 3 Gaps)
- Work environment doesn't support application of new skills
- Supervisor doesn't reinforce new behaviour
- Opportunity to practice is lacking
- Training was not designed around real job tasks
