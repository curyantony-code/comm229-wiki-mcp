# Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan, 2000)

## What It Is
A theory of human motivation proposing that motivation varies in *quality* (type), not just quantity (degree). The key distinction is between autonomous and controlled motivation.

## Motivation Continuum
```
Autonomous ←————————————————→ Controlled
Intrinsic | Identified | Introjected | External
```
| Type | Driver | Autonomy |
|---|---|---|
| **Intrinsic** | Enjoyment and genuine interest | Fully autonomous |
| **Identified** | Accepting value and importance of goals | Autonomous |
| **Introjected** | Guilt or self-pressure | Controlled |
| **External** | Reward or punishment | Fully controlled |

## Three Basic Psychological Needs
| Need | Desire | Need Satisfaction = |
|---|---|---|
| **Autonomy** | Choose one's own behaviour | Feelings of personal choice |
| **Competence** | Be effective in the world | Feelings of confidence and efficacy |
| **Relatedness** | Connect deeply with others | Feelings of belonging and closeness |

## Consequences

**Need Satisfaction → Autonomous Motivation → Positive Outcomes:**
Well-being · Performance · OCB · Adaptability · Creativity · Learning · Loyalty · Commitment · Engagement

**Need Frustration → Controlled Motivation → Negative Outcomes:**
Absenteeism · Turnover intention · Turnover · Aggression · Deviant work behaviour

## Manager Behaviours

### Autonomy-Supportive
- Offering genuine choices
- Explaining the *reasons* behind demands and rules
- Designing work to be interesting and meaningful

### Controlling (to avoid)
- Inducing guilt
- Using threats
- Manipulating using rewards or punishments
- Issuing orders without rationale

## Applications in COMM 229

| Context | How SDT applies |
|---|---|
| **Performance Management (wk8)** | GROW model coaching is autonomy-supportive; positive feedback builds competence |
| **Training (wk7)** | Explaining relevance of training activates identified motivation; Bandura's self-efficacy = competence need |
| **Job Design (wk2)** | JCM autonomy dimension satisfies SDT autonomy need; task significance satisfies relatedness |
| **Commitment (wk10)** | Affective commitment builds when autonomy, competence, relatedness are satisfied |
| **Health & Safety (wk11)** | Lack of autonomy and role ambiguity = need frustration = stress |

## Common Misconceptions
- External rewards don't automatically kill intrinsic motivation — it depends on *how* they're used (controlling vs. informational)
- SDT is about the *type* of motivation, not whether someone is motivated or not
- "More motivation" is not always better — controlled motivation with high intensity is still damaging
