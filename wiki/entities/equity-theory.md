# Adam's Equity Theory & Organizational Justice

## What It Is
A need/process theory of motivation proposing that people are motivated by perceptions of *fairness* — they compare their outcome-to-input ratio with a referent other.

## The Equity Formula
**My Outcomes/My Inputs** compared to **Referent's Outcomes/Referent's Inputs**

- **Inputs:** Skills, effort, reputation, performance, experience, hours worked
- **Outcomes:** Pay, promotions, recognition, interesting tasks, growth opportunities

## Inequity → Tension → Restoration

When perceived under-rewarded (or over-rewarded):

**Behavioural responses:**
- Change own inputs (work less or more)
- Change own outcomes (ask for raise)
- Change other's inputs or outcomes
- Quit

**Cognitive responses:**
- Reperceive own inputs or outcomes
- Reperceive other's inputs or outcomes
- Change the comparison person (referent)

## Organizational Justice (expansion)

| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Distributive justice** | Fairness of the *distribution* of outcomes — proportional to contribution |
| **Procedural justice** | Fairness of the *processes* used to determine outcomes (accurate, unbiased, correctable, consistent) |
| **Informational justice** | Adequacy of explanations and information provided about decisions |
| **Interpersonal justice** | Degree of politeness, dignity, and respect in treatment |

> **Key insight:** Procedural and interactional justice can buffer the impact of distributive injustice. Employees accept unfavourable outcomes better when the process is fair and they are treated with dignity.

## Applications in COMM 229
- **Compensation (wk9):** Pay equity (internal and external) — linked directly to distributive justice
- **Performance Management (wk8):** Appraisal processes must be procedurally fair (job-related, documented, appealable)
- **Commitment (wk10):** Perceived justice builds affective commitment and trust; injustice → exit/neglect (EVLN)
- **Recruitment (wk4):** Perceived fairness in hiring process affects employer brand

## Common Misconceptions
- Equity Theory is not about being paid equally — it's about the *ratio* of outcomes to inputs being perceived as equal to others'
- Over-reward can also create inequity tension (people may work harder or redefine their inputs)
- The referent other can be: a specific coworker, an industry average, one's own past experience
