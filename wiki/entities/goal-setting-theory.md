# Goal Setting Theory (Locke & Latham)

## What It Is
A process theory of motivation proposing that specific, challenging goals — combined with feedback — drive higher performance than vague or easy goals.

**Goals** = Internal representations of a desired state.

## SMARTER Goals
| Letter | Dimension | Question |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | Specific | What, how, where, when, with whom? |
| **M** | Measurable | How much, how well, at what cost? |
| **A** | Achievable | Challenging yet accepted (reasonable expectancy) |
| **R** | Relevant | Within one's control |
| **T** | Time-framed | Due date and when it will be assessed |
| **E** | Exciting | Goal *commitment*, not just compliance |
| **R** | Reviewed | Feedback and recognition on progress and accomplishment |

## Proximal vs. Distal Goals

| Type | Definition | When Most Important |
|---|---|---|
| **Proximal (sub-goals)** | Short-term, instrumental goals on the path to the distal goal | Novel and complex tasks |
| **Distal (long-term)** | Higher-order goals organizing multiple sub-goals | Always present as organizing structure |

**Together:** Proximal + distal goals have a significantly positive effect on strategy discovery, self-efficacy, and performance.

## Goal Orientation

| Orientation | Focus | Approach to Challenge |
|---|---|---|
| **Learning goals** | Process, skill development, internal standards | Adaptive; embrace challenge |
| **Performance goals** | Outcomes, demonstrating competence, comparison with others | May avoid challenge to protect image |

> Learning orientation leads to more adaptive behaviour; performance orientation can suppress risk-taking.

## Applications in COMM 229
- **Performance Management (wk8):** SMARTER goal-setting during performance planning; MBO is goal-setting operationalized at the org level; GROW coaching model focuses on goal (G) first
- **Training (wk7):** Training objectives use goal-setting principles (specific, measurable, conditions-based)
- **Motivation (wk9):** Expectancy theory: achievable goals → high expectancy; challenging goals → high valence of achievement
- **SDT connection:** Exciting (E) in SMARTER = goal commitment, not just compliance — reflects identified/intrinsic motivation vs. external compliance

## Common Misconceptions
- Specific challenging goals must also be *accepted* (achievable and relevant) — unrealistic goals destroy expectancy and commitment
- Feedback is part of the goal-setting system — goals without feedback lose much of their power
- SMARTER is not just SMART with two extra letters; E (exciting) and R (reviewed) address the motivational and dynamic elements often missing
