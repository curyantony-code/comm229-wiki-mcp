# Week 8: Performance Management

## Learning Objectives
1. Discuss how managing employee performance relates to organizational goals
2. Describe purposes of performance appraisals
3. Describe comparative and noncomparative appraisal methods
4. Discuss advantages/disadvantages of various raters
5. Describe guidelines for communicating performance feedback
6. Outline steps to creating a performance improvement plan
7. Explain how talent management uses performance and potential data

## Key Content

### Performance Management Systems
A set of integrated management practices that maximizes realization of employee potential and helps the organization meet its goals. Performance management is far broader than just the annual performance appraisal.

**Balanced Scorecard:** A measurement framework helping managers translate strategic goals into operational objectives for departments, teams, and individuals. Each goal should have a specific measure, target, and timeframe.

### Individual Performance = KSAOs + Motivation + Environment
- **Environment:** Culture/climate, supervisor relationship, colleagues, tools, equipment, constraints
- **KSAOs:** Knowledge, skills, and attitudes/other characteristics
- **Motivation:** Internal forces affecting voluntary choice of behaviour — direction, intensity, persistence

---

### Steps of an Effective Performance Management System
1. **Clarify work & set goals** — clear expectations, linked to org strategy, with employee participation
2. **Provide training & regular coaching** — show them how, reduce KSAO gap
3. **Provide frequent feedback** — recognize frequently; supportive and constructive as needed
4. **Create action plan** — if performance gap exists
5. **Recognize and reward performance** — financial and non-financial
6. **Conduct formal performance appraisal** — focus on goals, discuss improvement areas

---

### Clarify the Work — Goal Setting
**Performance objectives:** Targets that should be job-related, practical, and based on performance standards.
**Performance standards:** Measurable benchmarks relating to quality, quantity, or time.

**Communicating expectations:** Explain what *and* why. Show how it fits org goals. Connect to team and personal goals.

---

### Coaching
Partnering with employees in a thought-provoking and creative process to maximize their potential. Involves active listening and open-ended questions — not fault-finding.

**GROW Model:**
- **G**oal — What do you want to achieve?
- **R**eality — What is happening now?
- **O**ptions — What could you do?
- **W**ay forward — What will you do?

---

### Feedback

**Positive Feedback (Recognition):**
- Specific (describe the behaviour + details)
- Timely (soon after the behaviour)
- Frequent
- Focus on: competencies, effort, improvement, work practices, results

> **Exam tip:** The "feedback sandwich" (positive–negative–positive) makes the feedback giver feel better but results in confusion and reduced trust. Not recommended.

**Constructive Feedback (2-way Conversation):**
1. Approach & state purpose
2. Identify the issue (state expectations, describe issue objectively, respect effort, ask about causes)
3. Look for solutions (invite ideas, share yours, identify pros/cons, let person choose)
4. Elaborate action plan (set objectives together, explore obstacles, identify resources, set follow-up, express confidence)

---

### Performance Gap
**Reasons for Ineffective Performance:**
| Source | Examples |
|---|---|
| Organization | Insufficient training, ineffective practices, poor management |
| Job | Unclear expectations, lack of feedback, poorly designed job, inadequate equipment |
| Environment | Industry decline, legal constraints, union-mgmt conflict |
| Employee | Lack of KSAOs, low motivation, personal problems |

**Managing Ineffective Performance:** Target the source — offer training, transfer, modify working conditions, redesign job, etc.

---

### Performance Appraisal

**Sources of Information:**
- Direct vs. indirect
- Objective vs. subjective

**Raters:**
| Rater | Notes |
|---|---|
| Supervisor | Most common; often in best position |
| Subordinates | Useful for supervisory performance; must be confidential |
| Peers | Accurate for some behaviours; not useful in competitive environments |
| Self | Increases involvement; useful for development |
| Team | Useful when individual vs. team performance is hard to separate |
| Customers | Useful for customer service goals |
| 360-degree | All of the above combined |

**360-Degree Review Pros/Cons:**
- Pros: comprehensive, developmental, encourages accountability, flat-org compatible
- Cons: administratively complex, culture-dependent, requires training, conflicting opinions, confidentiality risks

**Rater Training:** Train on purpose, strategic alignment, forms, and biases.

---

### Comparative Appraisal Methods
Compare one employee's performance with coworkers.
- **Ranking method:** Best to worst (ordinal); subject to halo and recency effects
- **Forced distribution:** Sort employees into categories with fixed proportions (bell curve)

### Noncomparative Appraisal Methods
- **Graphic Rating Scale:** Oldest/most widely used; subjective; responses may be numerical
- **BARS (Behaviourally Anchored Rating Scale):** Descriptions of effective/ineffective performance placed along a scale; job-related, practical, standardized
- **BOS (Behaviour Observation Scale):** Similar to BARS but measures frequency of behaviour
- **MBO (Management by Objectives):** Employee evaluated on attainment of goals set jointly with supervisor

**MBO Process:** Corporate goals → Department goals → Individual goals (jointly agreed) → Interim reviews → Final review → Corporate performance review

---

### Rater Errors & Perceptual Biases
See [[entities/attribution-theory]] for attribution-related biases.

| Bias | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Halo effect** | One positive trait colours all evaluations |
| **Central tendency** | Rates everyone as average |
| **Leniency/strictness** | Rates everyone too high or too low |
| **Personal prejudice** | Bias based on non-performance characteristics |
| **Recency effect** | Recent events dominate the evaluation |
| **Primacy effect** | First impression dominates the evaluation |
| **Contrast errors** | Rating influenced by comparing to previous candidate/employee |
| **False-consensus effect** | Assumes others see/do things the same way |

> **Exam tip:** Recency = recent events dominate. Primacy = first impression dominates. Often confused.

---

### Attribution Theory
**Process:** Assign causes to explain behaviour — dispositional (internal) vs. situational (external) factors.

**Attribution Cues:**
- **Consistency:** How often does person engage in this behaviour in this situation?
- **Consensus:** How many other people engage in this behaviour in this situation?
- **Distinctiveness:** Does person engage in this behaviour in many situations?

**Attribution Pattern:**
- High consistency + Low consensus + Low distinctiveness → **Internal attribution**
- Low consistency + High consensus + High distinctiveness → **External attribution**

**Attribution Biases:**
- **Fundamental Attribution Error:** Overemphasize internal causes for *others'* actions
- **Self-Serving Bias:** Attribute own successes to internal factors; own failures to external factors

---

### Self-Fulfilling Prophecy (Pygmalion Effect)
1. Supervisor forms expectations about an employee
2. Expectations affect supervisor's behaviour toward employee
3. Supervisor behaviour affects employee's motivation and self-efficacy
4. Employee's behaviour becomes consistent with initial expectations

**Strongest when:** Employee is new · Multiple people hold same expectations · Employee has low achievement/self-esteem

**Prevention:** Can't rely on leader awareness alone. Need culture of support and learning. Hire optimistic supervisors.

---

### Legal Dimensions of Performance Appraisals
- A performance appraisal form is a **legal document**
- Use only job-related performance criteria
- Employees must receive written copy of performance standards before review
- Managers must be trained and able to observe performance
- Reviews must be documented, discussed openly, and may be appealed

## Exam-Relevant Highlights
- PM system is broader than appraisal (coaching, feedback, goal-setting, recognition)
- GROW model steps
- Feedback sandwich: not recommended despite popularity
- Comparative vs. noncomparative methods — BARS is job-related, practical, standardized
- MBO: jointly set goals; most employee-participatory method
- Attribution: internal (high consistency, low consensus, low distinctiveness) vs. external
- FAE vs. self-serving bias (direction of attribution error matters)
- Self-fulfilling prophecy: 4-stage cycle; strongest in new employees
- Performance appraisal = legal document
