# Week 7: Training and Development

## Learning Objectives
1. Explain key purposes and differences between onboarding, training, and development
2. List orientation and socialization aspects of onboarding
3. Describe the systems approach to training (instructional design)
4. Explain the different approaches to needs analysis
5. Explain the principles of learning
6. Define strategic human resource development
7. Discuss how to assess whether learning occurred (Kirkpatrick)
8. Describe how HR encourages career planning

## Key Content

### Introduction Distinctions
| Term | Definition | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Onboarding** | Helping new hires adjust quickly to performance and social aspects | Long-term |
| **Orientation** | Familiarizing new employees with org, job, unit, values | Short: 1–2 days |
| **Socialization** | Continuing process of integrating values, norms, and beliefs | Ongoing |
| **Training** | Acquiring KSAOs to perform *current* work | Focused |
| **Development** | Acquiring KSAOs to perform *future* work | Forward-looking |

**Learning = change in behaviour or knowledge.** All T&D processes increase human capital (competencies).

**Quebec Skills Development Act (Loi sur les compétences):** Organizations with payroll > $2M/year must spend 1% of payroll on training or pay 1% as tax. Unique in North America.

---

### Instructional Design — 4-Step Systems Approach
1. **Assess needs**
2. **Design & develop the program**
3. **Implement the program**
4. **Evaluate the program**

---

### Step 1: Assessing Needs
Determine gaps in current or future performance at 3 levels:
| Level | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Organizational** | Strategy, environment, resources, culture, constraints (what's the org about?) |
| **Task** | Expected performance — tasks, responsibilities, specific KSAOs required |
| **Employee** | Actual performance — actual KSAOs, barriers (lack of motivation, equipment, feedback, unclear expectations) |

> **Exam tip:** Needs assessment is the *most critical first step*. Choosing delivery method before assessing needs is a common error.

---

### Step 2: Designing the Program

**Training Objectives must specify:**
- Desired behaviour
- Performance criteria
- Conditions

**Principles of Learning:**
Goal setting · Repetition · Meaningful presentation (relevance) · Feedback · Individual differences · Active practice (participation) · Whole/part; Massed/distributed learning · Usefulness/Transference

**Learning Theories:**

**Bandura's Social Learning Theory:**
- We learn by observing others (models)
- More likely to imitate models who are similar, knowledgeable, recognized, rewarded, or caring
- We learn from observing consequences (reward or punishment)
- **Self-efficacy** — belief in one's abilities — influences whether we will imitate a model

**Kolb's Learning Cycle:**
See [[entities/kolb-learning-cycle]] for detail.
1. Concrete Experience → 2. Reflective Observation → 3. Abstract Conceptualization → 4. Active Experimentation → (back to 1)

**Delivery Methods:** On-the-job training · Apprenticeship · Cooperative/internship programs · Classroom instruction · Self-directed learning · Virtual/Augmented Reality · Digital learning · Seminars · Job rotation

**Learning Methods:** Lecture · Discussion · Case studies · Role-playing · Simulation · Special projects · Management games · Coaching

**Choosing Training Methods Based On:**
1. Cost-effectiveness
2. Desired program content
3. Appropriateness of facilities
4. Trainee preferences and capabilities
5. Trainer preferences and capabilities
6. Learning principles

> **Exam tip:** Simulation is most suitable for hands-on experience with real-life scenarios.

---

### Step 3: Implementing the Program
- Activate motivation: explain reasons and usefulness
- Demonstration and observation (PPTs, videos, modelling)
- Positive reinforcement
- Build on prior experience
- Reflection (what went well, what didn't, what to try differently)
- Spaced practice (spread learning to allow digestion and integration)

---

### Step 4: Evaluating the Program (Kirkpatrick's 4 Levels)
See [[entities/kirkpatrick-model]] for full detail.
1. **Reaction:** Trainee satisfaction — "Did they like it?"
2. **Learning:** Knowledge/skills change — "Did they learn?"
3. **Behaviour:** Transfer to job — "Did they apply it?" *(Common gap: training done but skills not applied)*
4. **Results (KPIs/ROI):** Organizational outcomes — "Did it improve business?"

> **Exam tip:** Behaviour (Level 3) is the level being assessed when employees are not applying new skills after training.

---

### Employee Development
**Definition:** Preparing employees to assume greater responsibilities and authority, often in formal leadership positions.

**Talent Management:** The practice of preparing a pool of developed employees to meet future organizational challenges.

**Development Plan Steps:**
1. Assess employees' needs
2. Link competencies to business goals
3. Identify learning and development activities
4. Determine available resources
5. Identify barriers to success

**Developmental Strategies:**
| Type | Focus | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| Cognitive | Alter thoughts/knowledge | Lectures, seminars, university education |
| Behavioural | Change behaviour/management style | Role-playing, behaviour modelling |
| Environmental | Provide developmental setting | Job rotation, temporary assignments, internal consulting |

---

### Career Development
The process by which employees explore and seek to align their career to their interests, needs, and motivations while meeting org goals.

**RIASEC Model (Holland's Vocational Interests):**
1. **Realistic:** Practical, methodological, assertive — work with things
2. **Investigative:** Thinking, observing, understanding — work with data
3. **Artistic:** Creative, sensitive, open — work with ideas and things
4. **Social:** Close relationships — work with people
5. **Enterprising:** Persuasive, value reputation/money/status — work with people and data
6. **Conventional:** Data, rules, structure, order — work with data

Tool: O*NET Interest Profiler

## Exam-Relevant Highlights
- Training (current work) vs. development (future work) distinction
- 3 levels of needs analysis (organizational, task, employee)
- Kirkpatrick's 4 levels — especially Level 3 (behaviour/transfer)
- Bandura's self-efficacy as predictor of learning
- Kolb's 4-stage learning cycle
- Quebec Skills Development Act (1% payroll)
- Simulation for hands-on learning
- RIASEC model — all 6 types
