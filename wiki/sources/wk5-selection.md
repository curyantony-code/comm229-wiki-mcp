# Week 5: Selection

## Learning Objectives
1. Describe the various steps in the selection process
2. Discuss types and usefulness of applicant screening tools
3. Explain the role of employment tests
4. Discuss major approaches to test validation
5. Outline steps in conducting an employment interview

## Key Content

### Selection Definition
The process of choosing individuals who have the relevant qualifications to perform well on a job. Challenge: getting information that is both **reliable** and **valid**.

### Reliability & Validity
- **Reliability:** The degree to which a measure provides *consistent* results over time
- **Validity:** The degree to which a measure *assesses what it is intended* to measure

> **Exam tip:** A tool can be reliable without being valid (consistently measures the wrong thing). High reliability does NOT guarantee high validity.

### Principles of Good Selection
1. Strategy success depends on employee calibre
2. Decisions must reflect job requirements
3. Must integrate with organizational priorities
4. Must recognize organizational constraints
5. Must recognize labour market realities (selection ratio)
6. Must be ethical

### Selection Process (Typical)
1. Reception of application form / resume
2. Initial screening interview (HR)
3. Employment tests
4. Interview with supervisor or team + Realistic Job Preview (RJP)
5. Reference check
6. Hiring decision

---

### Person-Environment Fit

**Person-Organization (PO) Fit:** Compatibility between employee and organizational characteristics (e.g., value congruence).

**Person-Job (PJ) Fit:**
- *Demands-abilities (DA) fit:* KSAs match job requirements
- *Needs-supplies (NS) fit:* Employee needs/preferences match job content

---

### Personal Values — Schwartz's Value Theory
See [[entities/schwartz-values]] for full detail.

10 values: Power · Achievement · Hedonism · Stimulation · Self-direction · Universalism · Benevolence · Conformity · Tradition · Security

Values in tension with each other form a circumplex (e.g., Stimulation vs. Security; Power vs. Universalism).

### Sources of Selection Information

#### 1. Application Forms & Resumes
- Quick, systematic; provide info on minimum requirements
- Basis for interview questions and reference checks
- Resumes: additional info on creativity/personality/interests

#### 2. Interviews
**Interview Methods:** One-on-one · Panel/group · Phone or Internet-based

**Types of Questions:**
| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Behavioural (BDI) | Past behaviour predicts future | "Tell me about a time when…" |
| Situational (SJT) | Hypothetical scenario | "How would you handle…" |
| Unstructured | No fixed format | Open-ended |

**Structured vs. Unstructured:**
- Structured: pre-determined questions; improves reliability and validity
- Unstructured: fewer planned questions; less reliable

**Interviewer Errors:** Halo effect · Leading questions · Stereotypes · Interviewer domination · Contrast errors
**Interviewee Errors:** Playing games · Talking too much · Boasting · Not listening · Unprepared

**Guidelines:** Plan interview · Establish rapport · Active listening · Attend to nonverbal cues · Provide honest info · Use questions effectively · Separate facts from inferences · Recognize biases · Standardize questions

#### 3. Tests
- Objective, standardized measures of KSAOs
- What you test must be related to the job

**Types:**
| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Cognitive Ability | Wonderlic, GMA |
| Personality | NEO-PI-R, Big 5 |
| Performance | Stromberg dexterity test |
| Integrity | Stanton survey |
| Substance Abuse | Drug testing |

**Big 5 Personality Traits (OCEAN):** See [[entities/big5-personality]]
- Openness to Experience · Conscientiousness · Extraversion · Agreeableness · Neuroticism

#### 4. Reference Checks
- Subjective picture of applicant; not highly predictive of performance
- Questions should be well-structured

---

### Selection Decision
**Subjective approach:** Personal judgment on collected information
**Statistical approach:** Multiple job-spec-based criteria weighted; candidate with highest composite score gets job; may use cut-offs

---

### Evaluating the Selection Process
1. **Yield ratios:** % of applicants proceeding to each stage
2. **Selection ratios:** # hired vs. # applicants
3. **Cost per hire**
4. **Time to hire:** Job advertisement to new employee start

---

### Realistic Job Preview (RJP)
A balanced picture of positive *and* negative aspects of the job and work context. Reduces turnover by managing expectations. First introduced as a recruitment tool (wk4) and used again in selection.

## Exam-Relevant Highlights
- Reliability vs. validity — reliably measuring the wrong thing is still invalid
- Structured interviews improve reliability and validity over unstructured
- BDI (past behaviour) vs. SJT (future scenario) — distinct question types
- Aligning behavioural questions to KSAOs increases validity without reducing reliability
- Big 5 traits and their high/low descriptors
- Schwartz values — especially tensions between adjacent vs. opposing values
- Selection ratio and yield ratio definitions
- PO vs. PJ fit distinction; DA vs. NS fit sub-types
