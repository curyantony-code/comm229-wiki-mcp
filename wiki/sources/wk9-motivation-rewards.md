# Week 9: Rewards — Financial & Nonfinancial

## Learning Objectives
1. Define motivation
2. Explain intrinsic and extrinsic motivation, and how to promote autonomous motivation
3. Outline expectancy theory and how to improve motivation
4. Explain how organizational justice influences motivation
5. Discuss the characteristics of effective goal setting

## Key Content

### Motivation Definition
1. The reason why somebody does something or behaves in a particular way
2. The feeling of wanting to do something, especially involving hard work and effort

**Three Dimensions:**
- **Direction:** The object of the effort (goal)
- **Intensity:** The level of effort
- **Persistence:** The duration of effort

### Need Theories vs. Process Theories
| Need Theories | Process Theories |
|---|---|
| Concerned with WHAT motivates | Concerned with HOW factors motivate |
| Needs = goals, wants, desires | Cognitions = mental events affecting energy and direction |
| Examples: Herzberg, SDT, Maslow | Examples: Equity Theory, Expectancy Theory, Goal Setting Theory |

## Self-Determination Theory (SDT)
**Deci & Ryan** — Proposes different *types* (quality) of motivation, not just degree.

### Types of Motivation (Continuum)
| Type | Description | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| **Intrinsic** | For enjoyment and interest | Autonomous |
| **Identified** | Accepting value and importance of goals | Autonomous |
| **Introjected** | Guilt or self-pressure | Controlled |
| **External** | To obtain reward or avoid punishment | Controlled |

> **Exam tip:** Autonomous motivation (intrinsic + identified) → beneficial outcomes. Controlled motivation (introjected + external) → negative outcomes.

### Three Basic Psychological Needs
1. **Autonomy:** Desire to choose one's behaviour → feelings of personal choice
2. **Competence:** Desire to be effective in the world → feelings of confidence and efficacy
3. **Relatedness:** Desire to be deeply connected with others → feelings of belonging and closeness

### Consequences of Need Satisfaction (Autonomous Motivation)
Well-being · Performance · OCB · Adapting to change · Creativity · Learning · Loyalty and trust · Commitment · Engagement

### Consequences of Need Frustration (Controlled Motivation)
Absenteeism · Turnover intentions · Turnover behaviour · Aggressivity · Deviant work behaviour

### Autonomy-Supportive vs. Controlling Behaviours
| Autonomy Supportive | Controlling |
|---|---|
| Offering choices | Inducing guilt |
| Explaining reasons behind demands/rules | Using threats |
| Designing interesting work | Manipulating with rewards/punishments |
| | Giving orders |

> **Exam tip:** A bonus tied to quotas + documentation of failure = controlled motivation (external regulation). This is the SDT-based explanation for why some incentive programs backfire.

**Dan Pink's related framework:** Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose as drivers of motivation.

## Expectancy Theory (Vroom)
**Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence**

| Component | Definition | Manager's Question |
|---|---|---|
| **Expectancy (E)** | Perceived probability that effort → desired performance level | Can I perform well if I put in effort? |
| **Instrumentality (I)** | Perceived probability that performance → desired outcomes | How likely is it that performing leads to outcomes? |
| **Valence (V)** | Perceived value of the outcomes | Are the outcomes desirable? |

If any component = 0 or near-zero, overall motivation collapses (multiplicative relationship).

**To increase motivation:** Increase expectancy (provide training, support) · Increase instrumentality (clarify performance-outcome links) · Increase valence (find valued rewards)

> **Exam tip:** "If I work hard I can perform well, but performance won't get me the promotion" → Low **Instrumentality**.

## Adam's Equity Theory
**Motivation stems from perceptions of inequity** (under-reward or over-reward) compared to a referent other.

**Formula:** My Outcomes/My Inputs vs. Referent's Outcomes/Referent's Inputs

**Restoring Equity (when feeling under-rewarded):**
- *Behavioural:* Change one's inputs/outputs; change other's inputs/outputs; quit
- *Cognitive:* Reperceive own or other's inputs/outputs; change comparison person

### Organizational Justice (expansion of equity)
| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Distributive justice** | Fairness of the distribution of outcomes (proportional to contribution) |
| **Procedural justice** | Fairness of processes used to determine outcomes (accurate, unbiased, correctable) |
| **Informational justice** | Adequacy of explanations provided about decisions |
| **Interpersonal justice** | Degree of politeness, dignity, and respect in treatment |

## Goal Setting Theory
**Goals** = Internal representations of a desired state. Specific, challenging goals with feedback drive high performance.

**SMARTER Goals:**
- **S**pecific — what, how, where, when, with whom
- **M**easurable — how much, how well, at what cost
- **A**chievable — challenging yet accepted (reasonable expectancy)
- **R**elevant — within one's control
- **T**ime-framed — due date and assessment timing
- **E**xciting — goal commitment, not just compliance
- **R**eviewed — feedback and recognition on progress

**Proximal vs. Distal Goals:**
- *Proximal:* Short-term sub-goals; instrumental for distal goal; especially important for novel/complex tasks
- *Distal:* Long-term higher-order goals; organizing and subsuming proximal goals
- Together they significantly improve strategy discovery, self-efficacy, and performance

**Goal Orientation:**
| Learning Goals | Performance Goals |
|---|---|
| Focus on learning process | Focus on achieving outcome |
| Developing skills | Demonstrating competence |
| Internal standard | Comparison with others |
| Adaptive behaviour pattern | May avoid challenge |

## Compensation & Benefits

### Total Compensation
Everything a company provides in exchange for work.

**Direct Compensation:** Wages, commissions, bonuses (based on critical job factors or performance)
**Indirect Compensation:** Benefits and services (not directly related to performance; 25–40% of payroll)

### Components of Total Compensation System
Compensation · Benefits · Social interaction · Security · Status/recognition · Work variety · Workload · Work importance · Authority/control · Advancement · Feedback · Work conditions · Development

### Objectives of Compensation
- **Internal equity:** Pay related to relative worth of jobs within the org
- **External equity:** Paying workers relative to the market

### Role of Benefits
**For organizations:** Recruit and retain, reduce fatigue, discourage labour unrest, satisfy employee objectives, minimize overtime
**For employees:** Lower costs, lower income taxes, inflation protection, supplemental health/life insurance

### Legally Required vs. Voluntary Benefits
| Legally Required | Voluntary |
|---|---|
| CPP/QPP | Life & Health Insurance |
| Employment Insurance | Disability Insurance |
| Workers' Compensation | Salary Continuation |
| Health Insurance Plans | Retirement Security |
| Holidays & Vacations | Paid Time-Off Benefits |
| | Employee Services |

### Total Rewards Approach Benefits
Easier recruitment of high-quality staff · Lower turnover · Higher employee performance · Enhanced employer reputation

## Exam-Relevant Highlights
- SDT: autonomous (intrinsic/identified) vs. controlled (introjected/external) — not just degree but TYPE
- Basic needs: autonomy, competence, relatedness
- Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence — multiplicative; any zero kills motivation
- "I can perform but won't get promoted" = low Instrumentality (frequently tested)
- Equity Theory: behavioural vs. cognitive restoration methods
- 4 types of organizational justice
- SMARTER goals — especially the E and R (often forgotten)
- Proximal + distal goals together outperform either alone
