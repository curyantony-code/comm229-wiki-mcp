# Week 1: Introduction to COMM 229

## Key Content

### Why People?
People are central to organizations — it takes people to get the work done. Focusing on people allows organizations to:
- Comprehend and predict workplace events
- Get things done through influence and coordination
- Promote engagement and build a skilled workforce
- Improve organizational effectiveness (the "bottom line")

### Core Definitions
- **Organizations:** Collective entities — groups working interdependently toward some purpose.
- **OB:** Studies what people think, feel, and do in and around organizations.
- **HRM:** Leadership and management of people using systems, methods, and processes that identify, select, motivate, and enable employees to achieve goals.

### HRM Activity Areas
Labour relations · Health & Safety · Org/Work/Job Design · HR Planning · Rewards & Recognition · Recruitment & Selection · Training & Development · Performance Management

### Strategic HRM
The process of integrating the strategic needs of an organization into the choice of HR systems and practices to support overall mission, strategies, and performance.

**5 Steps of Strategic HRM:**
1. Know what you are aligning to
2. Understand external and internal environment (Economic, Technological, Demographic, Cultural, Legal)
3. Make decisions
4. Impact HR practices
5. Evaluate decisions

> **Exam tip:** Strategic HRM requires a *clear line of sight* between HR strategy and corporate goals. Each HR practice should generate value for the organization.

### Foundations: Evidence-Based Management
HR decisions should be evidence-based. Barriers include: visual appeal of popular sources, perceptual errors, herd mentality. Anchors: practical, multidisciplinary, contingency, multiple levels of analysis.

### Authority Types in HR
- **Staff authority:** HR advises, does not direct (service role)
- **Line authority:** Operating managers make decisions
- **Functional authority:** HR given decision-making power in specific areas (e.g., benefits)

### Human Capital
Knowledge, skills, abilities, attitudes, and creativity employees bring. Competitive advantage because it's hard to find, copy, or replace. Improves org effectiveness directly.

### Downsizing Example
**Hidden costs of downsizing:** severance, pension payoffs, potential lawsuits, loss of institutional memory, loss of trust, lack of staffers when economy rebounds, risk-averse survivors.
**Alternatives:** reduce hours, job sharing, lower wages with shared ownership, transfers, attrition.

## Exercises & Cases
- Best/worst HR practice exercise (student reflection)
- "Senior VP HR considering plant in South America" — identify HR issues
- "Considering downsizing" — costs and alternatives

## Exam-Relevant Highlights
- Distinction between OB, HRM, and organizations
- Strategic HRM = alignment between HR practices and corporate strategy
- Staff vs. line vs. functional authority (often confused)
- Human capital as competitive advantage
- Manager vs. HR professional roles (distinct but complementary)
