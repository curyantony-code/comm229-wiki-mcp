## Learning Objectives

- Define OB, HRM, and the MARS model
- Explain perception, stereotypes, and attribution biases
- Identify Canadian employment law framework and DEI concepts
- Differentiate job satisfaction, commitment types, and stress reactions
- Classify motivation theories (needs vs. process) and apply Expectancy, Equity, and Goal-Setting theories

## Key Content

### Ch1 — Intro to OB

- **Organizations:** Groups working interdependently toward a common purpose
- **OB:** Study of what people think, feel, and do in/around organizations — purpose is to understand, predict, and influence organizational events
- **HRM:** Integrated set of systems, practices, and policies for effective deployment and development of employees
- **HRM topics:** Job design, recruitment & selection, orientation & training, performance management, rewards & compensation, labour relations, international HRM
- **Managers vs. HR professionals:** Managers = key link (people managers); HR = coordinates programs and equips managers
- **Organizational effectiveness:** Good fit with external environment; transforms inputs → outputs via human capital; satisfies stakeholders
- **Human capital:** Knowledge, skills, abilities, creative thinking employees bring
- **Corporate Social Responsibility (CSR):** Triple bottom line — profit + society + environment
- **Open Systems model:** Inputs → transforming → outputs; subsystems (accounting, production, marketing, culture, etc.)
- **5 Anchors of OB knowledge:** Systematic research, practical orientation, multidisciplinary, contingency, multiple levels of analysis
- **MARS Model:** Motivation + Ability + Role Perceptions + Situational Factors → Behaviour & Results (see [[entities/mars-model]])
- **Workplace trends:** DEI (surface-level = observable demographics; deep-level = psychological differences); work-life integration; remote work
- **Remote work:** Benefits (lower turnover, higher productivity) vs. Risks (social isolation, weaker culture); requires motivated employee + transportable job + performance-rewarding org

### Ch3 — Perception Process & Biases

- **Perception:** Interpreting messages of our senses to provide meaning
- **Selective attention:** Attending to some information while ignoring other
- **Confirmation bias:** Screening out contradictory information; accepting confirming information
- **Categorical thinking:** Organizing people/objects into preconceived categories (grouping by similarity, proximity, pattern)
- **Mental models:** Visual/relational images used to describe and predict; limitations → question assumptions, work with diverse people
- **Self-concept:** Individual's self-beliefs and self-evaluations; characteristics = complexity, consistency, clarity
  - Quadrants: social self, self-enhancement, self-evaluation, self-verification
  - **Locus of control:** Internal vs. external belief about control of behaviour
  - **Self-esteem:** Degree of positive self-evaluation
  - **General self-efficacy:** Belief in ability to succeed in challenging situations
- **Social Identity Theory:** People form self-perceptions based on character traits and group memberships
- **Stereotyping:** Characterizing a group by specific traits and auto-assigning to individuals
  - Why: categorization → homogenization → differentiation (also links to social identity + self-enhancement)
  - Problems: overgeneralizes; **stereotype threat** (sensitized to negative stereotype → increased effort to avoid it → more likely to display it); foundation of systemic discrimination
  - Key distinction: can minimize stereotype *application* but hard to prevent *activation*
- **Attribution Theory:** Assigning a cause to observed behaviour — see [[entities/attribution-theory]]
  - Dispositional vs. Situational attribution
  - 3 cues: **Consistency** (same behaviour over time?), **Consensus** (do others behave the same?), **Distinctiveness** (only in this situation?)
  - High consistency + low consensus + low distinctiveness → dispositional; High all three → situational
- **Attribution biases:**
  - **Fundamental Attribution Error (FAE):** Overemphasize dispositional explanations for *others'* behaviour
  - **Self-Serving Bias:** Credit successes internally; blame failures externally
  - **Actor-Observer Effect:** Actors attribute own behaviour situationally; observers attribute it dispositionally
  - **Self-Fulfilling Prophecy:** Expectations about a person cause that person to act consistently with expectations (4-stage cycle: supervisor forms expectations → affects supervisor behaviour → affects employee ability/motivation → employee behaviour confirms expectations)
  - **Halo Effect:** General impression of person based on one characteristic colours perception of others
  - **Primacy vs. Recency:** Perception biased toward first OR latest interactions
  - **False-Consensus Effect:** Overestimate how much others share our beliefs

### Ch9 — Laws & DEI

- **Canadian legal framework:** 2 distinct sets (federal + provincial); Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms = cornerstone; 14 jurisdictions; federal legislation applies to ~10% of workers (government departments, Crown corporations)
- **Discrimination:** Denying something based on race, ethnicity, gender, marital status, or other prohibited grounds
  - **Systemic discrimination:** Exclusion through application of policies not job-related
  - **Bona fide occupational qualification (BFOQ):** Job qualifications that may appear discriminatory but are justified by business/safety (e.g., bus driver, airline pilot)
  - **Duty to accommodate:** Employers must adjust practices to avoid discrimination
  - **Reverse discrimination:** Giving preference to certain groups such that others feel discriminated against
- **Harassment:** Any conduct a reasonable person would consider objectionable; includes bullying
  - **Psychological harassment:** Repeated, aggravating behaviour affecting dignity/integrity making work environment harmful
- **Employment Standards Legislation types:** Labour legislation, health & safety legislation, human rights legislation (paramount — protects from all harassment)
- **Employment equity:** Achieving equality in all aspects of employment; Employment Equity Act applies to federal employers with ≥100 employees
  - **Designated groups:** Women, visible minorities, Aboriginal peoples, persons with disabilities
- **Pay equity:** Equal pay for work of equal value
- **Diversity:** Combination of org policies supporting employee differences to reach business objectives
- **Inclusion:** Putting diversity into action
- **Ethics:** System of moral values motivating behaviour; **Whistleblowing** = reporting unethical behaviour outside the organization

### Ch4 — Work Attitudes & Stress

- **Emotions:** Physiological, behavioural, psychological episodes toward an object/person/event creating readiness
- **Attitudes:** Fairly stable tendency to respond consistently; Values + Beliefs = Attitudes → Behaviours
  - Easier to change beliefs than values; beliefs change with new information
- **Job satisfaction:** Collection of attitudes about one's job; overall = average of all facets
  - **Determinants:** Discrepancy (expectation vs. reality); Fairness (distributive/procedural/interactional); Disposition; Mood & Emotion
  - **Emotional contagion:** Moods and emotions spread to others
  - **Emotional regulation:** Requirements to conform to display rules at work
  - **Consequences:** Absenteeism, turnover, performance, OCB, customer satisfaction
- **Organizational Commitment:** Attitude toward one's organization
  - **Affective:** Based on identification — *want* to stay (internal)
  - **Continuance:** Based on cost of leaving — *have* to stay (external; e.g., escalating pay scale)
  - **Normative:** Based on obligation — *should* stay (moral)
  - **Fostering affective commitment:** Justice & support, shared values, organizational comprehension, trust, employee involvement
- **Workplace stress:** Adaptive emotional/physiological response to challenging or threatening situations
  - **Stressor → Stress → Stress Reaction**
  - **Stressors:** Organizational constraints, interpersonal conflict, work overload, low task control
  - **Stress reactions:**
    - *Behavioural:* Problem-solving, seeking social support, performance decrease, absenteeism/presenteeism, addictive substances
    - *Psychological (defense mechanisms):* Rationalization, projection, displacement, reaction formation, compensating
    - *Physiological:* Cardiovascular issues, diabetes, respiratory infections, weakened immune system
  - **Individual differences in stress:** Physical health/exercise, low neuroticism, positive self-concept (internal locus of control)
  - **Managing stress:** Remove stressor, withdraw, change stress perception, control consequence, receive social support

### Ch5 — Work Motivation

- **Motivation:** Extent to which an individual puts continuous effort toward a goal
- **Needs theories (content theories):** Explain what motivates (human needs); Needs → Behaviours → Goals
  - **McClelland:** Achievement (challenging tasks), Affiliation (maintain relationships), Power (influence others); non-hierarchical; see [[entities/mcclelland-needs-theory]]
- **Intrinsic vs. Extrinsic motivation:**
  - Intrinsic = from direct task relationship (autonomous motivation, SDT); Extrinsic = from work environment (controlled motivation, pay)
- **Self-Determination Theory (SDT):** Autonomous motivation > controlled; 3 basic needs: relatedness, competence, autonomy — see [[entities/self-determination-theory]]
- **Process theories:** Specify *how* motivation occurs
  - **Expectancy Theory (Vroom):** Effort → Performance (expectancy) → Reward (instrumentality) → Valued reward (valence); all three must be high — see [[entities/expectancy-theory]]
  - **Equity Theory:** Motivation from comparing own inputs/outputs ratio to others'; see [[entities/equity-theory]]
  - **Goal-Setting Theory:** Specific + Challenging + Commitment + Feedback = motivation; proximal (short-term sub-goal) vs. distal (long-term end-goal); goal orientation = individual's goal preferences — see [[entities/goal-setting-theory]]

> **Exam tip:** Needs theories = *content* theories (what motivates). Process theories = *how* motivation works. Expectancy, Equity, Goal-Setting are all process theories; McClelland is a needs/content theory.

### Ch2 — Job Analysis & Design

- **Job:** Group of related activities; **Position:** Duties performed by 1 employee; **Work:** Tasks to complete; **Role:** Part played by employee + expected behaviours
- **Job incumbent:** The employee hired to do a job
- **Job analysis:** Obtaining information about job duties, tasks, SKAs; sources = employees, supervisors, online profiles; methods = questionnaires, interviews, diaries, observations
- **Job description:** Lists tasks, duties, responsibilities, SKAs, competencies; sections = company name, position title, date, summary, duties, specification
- **Job design strategies:** Rotation, enlargement, enrichment (higher-order responsibilities), crafting (employee-driven), leadership teams
- **Job Characteristics Model (JCM):** Skill variety, task identity, task significance, autonomy, feedback → psychological states → performance & satisfaction — see [[entities/job-characteristics-model]]
- **Employee empowerment:** Involving employees to take charge of their work
- **Non-traditional arrangements:** Address flexibility needs (remote, part-time, gig)

## Exam-Relevant Highlights

> **Exam tip:** FAE = over-attributing *others'* behaviour dispositionally. Actor-Observer Effect = you (actor) blame situation; observer blames your disposition. These are frequently confused.

> **Exam tip:** Stereotype *activation* is hard to prevent (automatic); *application* is what can be minimized with awareness and effort.

> **Exam tip:** Attribution cues work in combination. High consistency + Low consensus + Low distinctiveness = dispositional. High all three = situational.

> **Exam tip:** BFOQ examples = airline pilot, bus driver (safety-based). Financial analyst is NOT a BFOQ job.

> **Exam tip:** Federal legislation covers only ~10% of Canadian workers. This number is frequently tested as a fill-in or MCQ.

> **Exam tip:** Affective = *want* (best for org performance); Continuance = *have to*; Normative = *should*. Continuance is increased by escalating pay, seniority benefits — cost of leaving.

> **Exam tip:** Stress sequence: Stressor → Stress (psychological reaction) → Stress Reaction (behavioural/psychological/physiological consequences). Students often swap stress and stress reaction.

> **Exam tip:** All three of Expectancy, Equity, Goal-Setting are *process* theories. McClelland's is a *needs* (content) theory.

> **Exam tip:** Job rotation = move between jobs. Job enlargement = more tasks same level. Job enrichment = higher responsibility. Job crafting = employee chooses own approach.

> **Exam tip:** JCM core dimensions — trick question: "task variety" is NOT one of them; it's *skill* variety.
