# Wize Mock Midterm 2

**Source:** Wizedemy Inc. — Mock Exam for COMM 229 Midterm 2  
**Format:** 20 multiple-choice questions covering all Midterm 2 topics

---

## Practice Questions with Answers & Rationale

**Q1.** A company analyzes past sales data and customer complaints to predict future staffing needs. Which method is the HR team using to forecast demand?  
→ **b) Trend analysis** — uses past numerical data to identify patterns for future demand forecasting (not Markov, which is supply-side)

**Q2.** A software company wants fresh ideas and specialized technical skills from outside. What advantage of external recruitment are they leveraging?  
→ **c) Acquiring people with specialized skills** — external recruitment brings in new ideas and specialized skills not available internally

**Q3.** An interviewer asks a candidate to describe a specific past situation where they resolved a team conflict. This is:  
→ **d) A behavioural description interview (BDI) question** — past-oriented ("describe a time when…"); situational = hypothetical future

**Q4.** A company wants to improve consistency of hiring results regardless of who conducts the interview. Which characteristic is being improved?  
→ **b) Reliability** — consistency of outcomes over time; validity = accuracy of measurement

**Q5.** A police department tests candidates' ability to run, carry heavy loads, and climb stairs. This assessment is:  
→ **e) Physical ability test** — testing physical capacity required by the job; considered a valid measure

**Q6.** A hotel manager notices front desk and banquets teams each have their own unique culture. What is observed?  
→ **c) Subcultures within the organization** — smaller cultures formed based on department/occupation; not "strong culture" (that refers to intensity, not fragmentation)

**Q7.** Employees resist a new project management system because they fear the unknown and are unsure how it will affect their roles. Most likely reason:  
→ **a) Negative valence towards change and fear of the unknown** — two of the three main resistance causes

**Q8.** During a merger, HR conducts a cultural assessment to understand potential value clashes. This process is:  
→ **c) Bicultural audit** — diagnoses cultural relations to determine extent of clash; NOT organizational socialization

**Q9.** A multinational invites 300+ employees to a structured workshop to co-create solutions for the organization's future. Which change approach?  
→ **c) Large Group Intervention (LGI)** — engages a significant portion of the org in shaping its future; participative mass-change method

**Q10.** Employees share stories about how the CEO personally resolved a customer complaint. What is the manager observing?  
→ **c) Stories** — spoken narratives passed between employees depicting values and what is accepted; NOT rituals (recurring events) or symbols (artifacts)

**Q11.** Which best describes onboarding?  
→ **b) Helping new hires adjust quickly to both job tasks and social aspects of the organization** — onboarding = performance + social adjustment; NOT just skill acquisition

**Q12.** A company wants to prepare an employee for a managerial role in five years. Which approach is most appropriate?  
→ **c) Development** — future-oriented; training = current job; orientation = at hiring

**Q13.** A manager observes employees applying new skills from training directly on the job with measurable improvements. Which Kirkpatrick level?  
→ **c) Behaviour** — Level 3 = transfer of training (applying learned skills on the job); Learning = Level 2 (knowledge acquired); Results = Level 4 (ROI/org impact)

**Q14.** A company first assesses employee skills then identifies learning opportunities aligned with business goals. Which step of employee development planning?  
→ **d) Linking competencies and skills to business goals** — Step 2 of the 5-step development plan (after assessing needs)

**Q15.** HR discovers managers rarely meet employees, employees feel the system is unfair, and annual reviews dominate. Which reasons best explain PM failure?  
→ **d) A and B** — Objectives not prioritized (A) + employees not involved in goal-setting / managers dislike feedback discussions (B); both are in the 17-point failure list

**Q16.** An IT company implements a 360-degree feedback system (self, peers, subordinates, customers). What is the main purpose of combining these sources?  
→ **c) To gather a well-rounded view of employee performance for both development and administrative purposes** — 360 serves both developmental and administrative functions

**Q17.** A 360-degree review combines feedback from multiple sources for comprehensive assessment. What is the main purpose of a performance management system?  
→ **d) To plan for employee growth, pay, and document decisions** — PM serves compensating, planning, documenting, and coaching purposes

**Q18.** Emma's manager rates her 1–5 on enthusiasm, creativity, and dependability. Which review method?  
→ **c) Trait method using a graphic rating scale** — rates traits on a numerical scale; BARS = scales per performance dimension (more behavioural/job-specific)

**Q19.** Liam and his manager agree on specific quarterly goals; performance is measured against whether he achieved them. Which method?  
→ **d) Management by objectives (Results method)** — employee & manager agree on individual goals; assessed against results achieved

**Q20.** A company encourages risk-taking, experimenting, few rules, and low cautiousness. Which organizational culture dimension?  
→ **b) Innovation** — defined by experimenting, opportunity-seeking, risk-taking, few rules (vs. Stability = predictability, rules; Aggressiveness = competitive)

## Instructor Notes

> **Instructor note:** Questions 1, 6, 8, 9, 10 are the highest-error questions for students — they require distinguishing between closely related concepts (trend analysis vs. Markov; subcultures vs. strong culture; bicultural audit vs. socialization; LGI vs. appreciative inquiry; stories vs. rituals). Worth dedicating exam prep time to these.

> **Instructor note:** Questions 12–14 test the Onboarding/Training/Development and Kirkpatrick continuum. Students frequently confuse Training (current job) and Development (future role), and misidentify Kirkpatrick Level 3 (behaviour/transfer) vs. Level 2 (learning).

> **Instructor note:** Questions 18–20 test performance review method distinctions. BARS vs. graphic rating scale vs. MBO and culture dimension vocabulary (innovation ≠ aggressiveness) are recurring MCQ traps.
