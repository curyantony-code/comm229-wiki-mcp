# Week 4: Planning & Recruitment

## Part 1: HR Planning

### Learning Objectives
1. Explain the importance of HR plans for strategic success
2. Describe the HR planning process
3. Discuss methods for estimating demand & supply
4. Identify solutions to shortages or surpluses

### HR Planning Definition
The process of anticipating current and future needs to ensure people are used effectively to accomplish organizational goals. Seeks to have **the right people with the right skills in the right jobs at the right time.** Implies forecasting demand *and* supply.

### Levels of Planning Sophistication
| Level | Characteristics |
|---|---|
| No formal planning | Reactionary; common in small companies |
| Basic planning | Mix of proactive/reactive; 1–2 year horizon |
| Advanced planning | Direct tie to strategy; 3–5 year horizon |
| Sophisticated planning | HR integral to strategy; 5+ years; strong analytics |

### HR Planning Steps
1. Forecast demand
2. Assess supply
3. Develop objectives to eliminate the gap
4. Design & implement systems
5. Evaluate

---

### Forecasting Demand
**Drivers:** Demographic impacts · Turnover · Legal changes · Technological changes · Competitors · Budgets and revenue forecasts · New ventures

**Techniques:**
| Technique | Description |
|---|---|
| Expert forecast | Managers' judgment — informal decisions, formal survey, Delphi technique |
| Trend analysis | Past data (production, etc.) to project needs — extrapolation, indexation, statistical analysis |
| Data analytics | Simulation and predictive models combining multiple inputs |
| Other sources | Budget/planning analysis, new-venture analysis, staffing tables |

> **Exam tip:** Trend analysis = past production/employee ratios. Expert forecast = opinion-based. Data analytics = algorithmic. Know the difference.

---

### Assessing Supply

**Internal Supply Tools:**
- HR audits: skill inventories (KSAOs), management inventories
- Staffing tables
- **Transition matrices / Markov analysis:** Model expected movement of employees across jobs using past data

**Markov Analysis Example:**
A transition probability matrix shows (e.g.) that 80% of Job A employees stay in Job A, 10% move to Job B, 5% move to Job C, 5% exit. Multiply by current staffing levels to get predicted end-of-year staffing.

**External Supply Tools:**
- Labour market analysis
- Demographic trends (e.g., attractiveness of occupations, regional supply)

---

### Eliminating the Gap

**Strategies for Oversupply (too many people):**
- Attrition (hiring freeze, early/phased retirement)
- Headcount reduction (layoffs, leave without pay, voluntary separation incentives, termination)
- Alternative work arrangements (job sharing, part-time)

**Strategies for Shortage (too few people):**
- Hire employees (full-time, part-time, temporary)
- Source service providers (independent contractors, outsourcing, crowdsourcing)
- Develop employees (succession planning, career development, transfers)
- Work arrangements (overtime, flexible schedules)

---

### HRIS (Human Resource Information System)
Used to collect, record, store, analyze, and retrieve HR data. Major stakeholders: HR professionals, managers, employees.

**Why use HRIS?**
- Increased efficiency (enhanced service delivery)
- Increased effectiveness (better decisions)
- Increased organizational sustainability (talent management)
- Increased visibility (enhanced HR competencies)

## Part 2: Recruitment

### Learning Objectives
1. Explain the recruitment function process
2. Describe the two most common methods of applying
3. Describe how to choose among recruitment methods

### Recruitment Definition
The process of locating and encouraging potential applicants to apply for jobs. Can be done from within and/or outside the organization.

### Recruitment Process Steps
1. Identify current/future openings (based on HR planning)
2. Review job specifications (KSAOs)
3. Determine recruitment method(s) + internal vs. external
4. Create a pool of qualified applicants
5. Maintain applicants' interest
6. Persuade selected candidates to accept

### Employer Branding
What applicants believe their employment experience will be like: work-life balance, fairness & equity, communication norms, recognition, culture.

---

### Internal Recruitment
**Pros:** Rewards past performance · Employees know the org · Motivates employees seeking promotion
**Cons:** May lack required training/experience · Risk of perpetuating same ideas · Harder to change culture

**Methods:** HR management systems (databases) · Succession planning · Internal job postings

---

### External Recruitment
**Pros:** Unique skills · More variety of experience and perspectives · May increase innovation
**Cons:** Lack of first-hand performance info · New employee unfamiliar with org · More expensive

**Methods:** Direct inquiries · Employee referrals · Advertising (newspaper, TV, billboard, professional journals) · Digital/Social media recruiting · ESDC · Private employment agencies · Professional search firms · Educational institutions · Professional/labour organizations · Canadian Forces · Temporary-help agencies · Departing/past employees · Job fairs · Contract workers · International recruitment

---

### Labour Market
Geographical area from which the organization recruits. Varies by job type, requirements, salary, and local labour supply.

### Choosing Recruitment Methods
Ask: How many recruits? Skill level? Available sources? Past effectiveness? Budget? Labour agreements?

> **Exam tip:** Internal recruitment narrows inflow of new knowledge and perspectives — explains why heavy reliance on it reduces diversity and innovation.

## Exercises & Cases
- **SnowPeak Apparel:** Trend analysis — forecast production employees, calculate hires needed given turnover
- **RecruitRight:** For each client, select methods + recommend attractive vs. realistic messaging
- **OmniVehicle case:** Diagnose reduced applications, recommend internal vs. external for team leads

- Markov/transition matrix — how to read and apply
- Oversupply vs. shortage strategies
- Internal vs. external recruitment pros/cons
- Labour market concept
- Employer branding and realistic job preview (RJP) — balances attractive and realistic info
