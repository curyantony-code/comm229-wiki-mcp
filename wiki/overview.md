# COMM 229: Managing People in Organizations

**Institution:** Concordia University | **Textbook:** Schwind, 13th Edition

## Course Purpose
To develop students' understanding of how to manage and lead people effectively within organizations, integrating Organizational Behaviour (OB) and Human Resources Management (HRM) perspectives.

## Key Definitions
- **Organizations:** Collective entities — groups working interdependently toward some purpose.
- **Organizational Behaviour (OB):** Studies what people think, feel, and do in and around organizations.
- **Human Resources Management (HRM):** Leadership and management of people using systems, methods, and processes to identify, select, motivate, and enable employees to achieve outcomes that enhance organizational goals.

## Central Thesis
People are a competitive advantage. Human capital — the knowledge, skills, abilities, and attitudes employees bring — is difficult to copy or replace. Every HRM function exists to attract, develop, motivate, and retain human capital in service of organizational strategy.

> **Instructor note:** The course consistently returns to three questions: (1) What should organizations do? (2) Why does it matter? (3) How do HR and line managers share responsibility?

## Manager–HR Partnership
| Line Managers | HR Professionals |
|---|---|
| Determine work/job design | Align HR strategy with business strategy |
| Make hiring decisions | Deliver services (recruiting, policy) |
| Coach and develop employees | Are employee advocates |
| Supervise day-to-day | Assist managers with HR issues |
| Evaluate performance | Develop and implement HR policies |
