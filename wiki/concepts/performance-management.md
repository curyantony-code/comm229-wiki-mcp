# Performance Management

## Scope
Performance management is a set of integrated management practices that:
1. Maximizes the realization of employee potential
2. Helps the organization meet its goals

It encompasses: goal-setting, coaching, feedback, recognition, performance appraisal, and development planning — not just the annual review.

## Performance = KSAOs + Motivation + Environment
All three must be addressed. A gap in any one leads to performance problems.

## The PM Cycle (6 Steps)
1. **Clarify work & set goals** — linked to strategy, with employee participation
2. **Training & coaching** — reduce KSAO gap, show them how
3. **Frequent feedback** — recognize frequently; constructive as needed
4. **Action plan** — address performance gaps
5. **Recognize & reward** — financial and non-financial
6. **Formal appraisal** — focus on goals, discuss improvement areas

---

## Goal Setting & Expectations
- **Performance objectives:** Job-related, practical, based on performance standards
- **Performance standards:** Measurable benchmarks (quality, quantity, time)
- Employees must know the *what* and the *why* — connect to org goals and personal goals

**Goal Setting Theory applies:** SMARTER goals. Proximal + distal goals together improve performance. See [[entities/goal-setting-theory]].

---

## Coaching (GROW Model)
A thought-provoking, creative partnership — not fault-finding. Uses active listening and open-ended questions.

| Step | Question |
|---|---|
| **G**oal | What do you want to achieve? |
| **R**eality | What is happening now? |
| **O**ptions | What could you do? |
| **W**ay forward | What will you do? |

## Feedback

### Positive Feedback
- Specific, timely, frequent
- Focus on: competencies, effort, improvement, work practices, results
- ❌ **Feedback sandwich** — not recommended; creates confusion and erodes trust

### Constructive Feedback (2-way conversation)
1. Approach and state purpose
2. Identify issue (state expectations, describe objectively, ask about causes)
3. Look for solutions (invite employee ideas, share yours, identify pros/cons)
4. Elaborate action plan (objectives, obstacles, resources, follow-up, confidence)

**Theoretical basis:** SDT (autonomy-supportive), Expectancy Theory (competence-building), Goal Setting (specific targets).

## Performance Gap Analysis
**Sources of ineffective performance:**
- Organization: insufficient training, ineffective practices, poor management
- Job: unclear expectations, lack of feedback, poorly designed job
- Environment: industry decline, legal constraints
- Employee: lack of KSAOs, low motivation, personal problems

**Management response:** Target the source. Training ≠ always the answer.

> **Instructor note:** If poor performance is widespread, the problem may be in *HR systems* (poor selection, inaccurate job analysis), not the employees.

## Appraisal Methods

### Comparative Methods (employee vs. employee)
| Method | Description | Limitation |
|---|---|---|
| **Ranking** | Best to worst (ordinal) | Subject to halo and recency effects |
| **Forced distribution** | Fixed proportions into categories (bell curve) | Arbitrary; demoralizing in small teams |

### Noncomparative Methods (employee vs. standard)
| Method | Description | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Graphic Rating Scale** | Subjective; oldest and most common | Prone to rater errors |
| **BARS** | Behavioural anchors on a scale; job-related, practical, standardized | Most defensible legally |
| **BOS** | Like BARS but measures *frequency* of behaviour | More objective than BARS |
| **MBO** | Evaluated on jointly-set goal attainment | Most participatory |

## Rater Sources
Supervisor · Subordinates · Peers · Self · Team · Customers (internal/external) · 360-degree (all combined)

**360-degree:** Comprehensive and developmental but complex, culture-dependent, requires training.

**Rater training must cover:** Purpose, strategic alignment, forms, and biases.

---

## Rater Errors (Perceptual Biases)
| Bias | Description |
|---|---|
| **Halo effect** | One positive trait colours all judgments |
| **Primacy effect** | First impression dominates |
| **Recency effect** | Recent events dominate the evaluation |
| **Central tendency** | Rates everyone as average |
| **Leniency/Strictness** | Rates everyone too high or too low |
| **Contrast errors** | Rating influenced by comparison to others |
| **Personal prejudice** | Non-performance-based bias |
| **False-consensus** | Assumes others see/do things the same way |

## Attribution Theory in Performance Management
See [[entities/attribution-theory]] for full detail.

**Fundamental Attribution Error:** Supervisors over-attribute employee failure to internal factors (disposition) and ignore situational causes.

**Self-Serving Bias:** Employees attribute their success internally and failure externally.

**Practical implication:** When diagnosing a performance gap, supervisors must investigate situational factors before concluding an employee "isn't trying hard enough."

---

## Self-Fulfilling Prophecy
Supervisor expectations shape employee behaviour:
1. Supervisor forms expectations
2. Expectations affect supervisor behaviour
3. Supervisor behaviour affects employee motivation/self-efficacy
4. Employee behaviour matches expectations

**Prevention:** Hire optimistic supervisors; build culture of support and learning; don't rely on leader awareness alone.

---

## Legal Compliance
- Appraisal form is a **legal document**
- Use only job-related performance criteria
- Employees must get written standards before review
- Must be documented, discussed openly, and appealable
