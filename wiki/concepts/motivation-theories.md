# Motivation Theories

## Overview
Motivation = the reason why someone does something; the feeling of wanting to do something involving hard work and effort.

**Three dimensions:** Direction (goal) · Intensity (level of effort) · Persistence (duration of effort)

**Two families of theory:**
| Need Theories | Process Theories |
|---|---|
| WHAT motivates people | HOW motivation works |
| Herzberg, SDT, Maslow | Equity, Expectancy, Goal Setting |

---

## Self-Determination Theory (Deci & Ryan)

**Core insight:** Motivation varies in *type* (quality), not just degree.

### Motivation Continuum
```
Intrinsic → Identified → Introjected → External
[Autonomous ←————————→ Controlled]
```
- **Intrinsic:** Enjoyment and interest — fully autonomous
- **Identified:** Accept the value and importance — autonomous
- **Introjected:** Guilt or self-pressure — controlled
- **External:** To obtain reward or avoid punishment — controlled

### Three Basic Psychological Needs
- **Autonomy:** Choose one's own behaviour → feelings of choice
- **Competence:** Be effective → feelings of confidence and efficacy
- **Relatedness:** Connect with others → feelings of belonging

**Need satisfaction → autonomous motivation → positive outcomes**
(Well-being, performance, OCB, creativity, engagement, loyalty)

**Need frustration → controlled motivation → negative outcomes**
(Absenteeism, turnover, aggression, deviant behaviour)

### Manager's Toolkit
**Autonomy-supportive:** Offer choices, explain reasons, design interesting work
**Avoid:** Guilt, threats, manipulation through rewards/punishments, orders

## Expectancy Theory (Vroom)

**Formula:** Motivation = Expectancy × Instrumentality × Valence (multiplicative)

| Component | Question | Manager lever |
|---|---|---|
| **Expectancy (E)** | Can I perform well if I try? | Train, support, build self-efficacy |
| **Instrumentality (I)** | If I perform, will I get outcomes? | Clarify and deliver performance-outcome links |
| **Valence (V)** | Are outcomes desirable to me? | Individualize rewards |

**Implication:** If any component is zero, motivation = zero. All three must be addressed.

> **Common exam scenario:** "I can perform, but performance won't get me a promotion" → Low **Instrumentality**.

---

## Adam's Equity Theory

**Core:** People compare their outcome/input ratio to a referent other's ratio.

**Inequity → tension → motivation to restore equity**

**Restoration methods:**
- *Behavioural:* Change own inputs or outputs; change other's inputs/outputs; quit
- *Cognitive:* Reperceive own or other's inputs/outputs; change who you compare to

**Organizational Justice (expansion):**
- **Distributive justice:** Fairness of outcome distribution
- **Procedural justice:** Fairness of processes used (accurate, unbiased, correctable)
- **Informational justice:** Adequacy of explanations
- **Interpersonal justice:** Politeness, dignity, respect

---

## Goal Setting Theory (Locke & Latham)

**Core:** Specific, challenging goals + feedback = high performance

**SMARTER Goals:**
S — Specific | M — Measurable | A — Achievable | R — Relevant | T — Time-framed | E — Exciting | R — Reviewed

**Proximal + distal goals:** Sub-goals (proximal) + long-term goals (distal) together improve strategy discovery, self-efficacy, and performance. Critical for novel/complex tasks.

**Goal Orientation:**
- *Learning goals:* Focus on process, developing skills, internal standard → adaptive
- *Performance goals:* Focus on outcomes, demonstrating competence, comparison with others → can avoid challenge

## Integrating the Theories

| Level | What matters | Which theory |
|---|---|---|
| Why the person tries | Autonomous vs. controlled motivation | SDT |
| Whether effort → performance | Self-efficacy, training, resources | Expectancy (E component) |
| Whether performance → outcomes | Clear reward links, trustworthy management | Expectancy (I component) |
| Are outcomes worth it? | Reward design, individualization | Expectancy (V) + Equity |
| Are process and treatment fair? | Procedural and interpersonal justice | Equity/Organizational Justice |
| Are goals clear and challenging? | Goal specificity and difficulty | Goal Setting |
