# Diversity, Equity & Inclusion (DEI)

## Core Definitions
- **Diversity:** Recognizing the *presence* of differences among employees
- **Equity:** Ensuring *access to the same opportunities*
- **Inclusion:** *Welcoming and valuing* all people

**Workplace diversity** refers to differences among employees (potential or actual). Employment equity is **mandatory**; diversity management is **voluntary**.

## Benefits of Diversity
- Access to larger candidate pool; more creative decisions; more innovation
- Better understanding of customer needs; more positive org image
- More positive employee attitudes; better retention
- Savings in litigation
- Higher company performance and competitiveness

## Challenges of Diversity

### Similarity-Attraction Phenomenon
Tendency to be more attracted to people who are similar to us. Creates a bias where surface-level diversity (gender, ethnicity, age) is assumed to correlate with deep-level diversity (values, attitudes, beliefs).

### Ethnocentrism
The belief that one's own culture is superior to other cultures. Undermines fair treatment of diverse employees.

### Stereotyping
Grouping people based on commonalities without consideration of individual capabilities.

### Glass Ceiling
Invisible but real obstructions to career advancement of women and visible minorities.

### Old Boy's Network
Informal relationships among male managers and executives that exclude women and minorities.

### Pet to Threat
Shared experience by many Black women where mentors/managers who once supported them later undermine them when they are perceived as competition.

## Managing Diversity
- Make leaders **accountable** for diversity
- Build a **culture that embraces diversity**
- Establish **diversity training programs**
- **Review HR practices** (communication, selection, development)
- Be **systematic:** assess needs, set goals, monitor progress

## DEI Initiatives
| Initiative | Description |
|---|---|
| **Inclusive internal systems** | Communication, hiring, reward structures that promote inclusion |
| **DEI training** | Awareness training; skill-building training; content vs. process training |
| **Alternate work arrangements** | Non-traditional arrangements; more flexibility |
| **Apprenticeships** | On-the-job training with experienced person |
| **Support groups** | Emotional support for employees sharing common attributes |
| **Communication standards** | Formal internal protocols to eliminate communication biases |

## Connection to Employment Equity
Employment equity targets four designated groups: women, First Nations peoples, visible minorities, people with disabilities. Goal: equitable representation in the workforce. This is mandatory for federally regulated employers.
