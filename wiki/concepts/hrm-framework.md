# HRM Framework

## Definition & Scope
HRM is the leadership and management of people within an organization using systems, methods, processes, and procedures that identify, select, motivate, and enable employees to achieve outcomes that enhance the organization's goals.

OB (Organizational Behaviour) is the scientific foundation — it studies what people think, feel, and do in and around organizations. HRM translates OB insights into management practice.

## HRM Activity Areas
Labour relations · Health & Safety · Organizational/Work/Job Design · HR Planning · Rewards & Recognition · Recruitment & Selection · Training & Development · Performance Management

## Strategic HRM
**Definition:** The process of integrating the strategic needs of an organization into the choice of HR systems and practices to support overall mission, strategies, and performance.

**Key principle:** There must be a *clear line of sight* between HR strategy and corporate goals. Every HR practice must generate value.

**5 Steps:**
1. Know what you are aligning to (corporate goals)
2. Understand external + internal environment (ETDCL: Economic, Technological, Demographic, Cultural, Legal)
3. Make decisions (HR choices)
4. Impact HR practices (implement)
5. Evaluate decisions

**Evidence-based management:** HR decisions should be grounded in evidence (multidisciplinary, practical, contingency-based, multi-level).

## Authority in HR
| Type | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Staff authority** | HR advises, does not direct — service role |
| **Line authority** | Operating managers make decisions |
| **Functional authority** | HR given specific decision-making power (e.g., type of benefits) |

## Manager–HR Professional Partnership
| Line Managers | HR Professionals |
|---|---|
| Determine work/job design | Align HR strategy with business strategy |
| Make hiring decisions | Deliver services (recruiting, policy) |
| Coach and develop employees | Develop and implement HR policies |
| Supervise day-to-day | Are employee advocates |
| Evaluate performance | Assist managers with HR issues |

## Human Capital
Knowledge, skills, abilities, attitudes, and creativity employees bring. Competitive advantage because it is hard to find, copy, or replace with technology. Improves org effectiveness directly through better individual behaviour and performance.
