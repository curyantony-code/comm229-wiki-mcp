# Canadian Employment Law

## Overview
Four bodies of employment legislation govern HR in Canada:
1. **Employment Standards Laws** — minimum obligations
2. **Human Rights Laws** — non-discrimination
3. **Privacy Laws** — personal information
4. **Equity Laws** — pay and employment equity

Plus OHS (Occupational Health & Safety) legislation — covered in [[sources/wk11-health-safety]].

> **Key principle:** Ignorance of the law is not a valid excuse. HR must determine which laws apply and ensure compliance.

## Which Laws Apply — 3-Question Framework
1. **Employee or independent contractor?** (Control, tools ownership, profit/loss risk)
2. **Federal or provincial jurisdiction?** (~10% federal — banks, airlines, rail, telecom; ~90% provincial)
3. **Unionized or non-unionized?** (Labour relations acts vs. employment standards acts)

## Employment Standards
**Purpose:** Specify minimum employer obligations.
**Content:** Hours of work, minimum wages, overtime pay, vacation pay, statutory holidays.
**Quebec enforcer:** CNESST (Commission des normes, de l'équité, de la santé et de la sécurité du travail)

## Human Rights Legislation

### Protected Grounds (Canadian Human Rights Act)
Ethnicity · Race · Ethnic/national origin · Religion · Age · Sex · Sexual orientation · Marital status · Family status · Disability status · Pardoned offences

### Types of Discrimination
| Type | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct** | Adverse treatment based on protected ground | Refusing to hire based on religion |
| **Indirect/Systemic** | Policy with discriminatory *effect* even if not intent | Height minimums excluding women |
| **Reverse** | Preference given to protected group over equally qualified non-member | Controversial — see employment equity |

### BFOQ & Duty to Accommodate
- **BFOQ:** Discrimination is legally permitted if the employer can prove business necessity
- **Duty to accommodate:** Employer must adjust policies/practices to prevent discrimination, up to *undue hardship*

### Harassment
**Psychological harassment — 5 criteria:**
1. Vexatious behaviour
2. Repetitive in nature
3. Verbal comments or gestures that are hostile and unwelcomed
4. Affects dignity or integrity
5. Creates a harmful work environment

**Sexual harassment:** Unsolicited or unwelcome sex/gender-based conduct with adverse employment consequences.

**Employer retaliation:** Criminal act. Enforcement: CHRC or provincial human rights bodies.

## Equity Legislation
| Law | Definition |
|---|---|
| **Pay Equity** | Equal pay for work of *equal value* — eliminates gender wage gap |
| **Employment Equity** | Mandatory process for four designated groups: women, First Nations peoples, visible minorities, people with disabilities |

> **Key distinction:** Employment equity (mandatory) ≠ diversity management (voluntary).

## Privacy Legislation
**PIPEDA:** Sets rules for how organizations collect, use, and disclose personal information.
**Privacy Act:** Right to access and correct personal information the Government of Canada holds about you.

## OHS Laws (brief — see wk11 for full detail)
**Three fundamental employee rights:**
1. Right to know about hazards
2. Right to participate in correcting hazards
3. Right to refuse dangerous work

**Key:** Due diligence is best defence against OHS offences.

## Cross-HR-Function Impact
| HR Function | Legal Constraints |
|---|---|
| Recruitment | No discriminatory job postings; protect privacy of applicants |
| Selection | Tests and interviews must be valid and non-discriminatory; BFOQ applies |
| Job descriptions | Specs must be bona fide; avoid irrelevant requirements |
| Performance appraisal | Legal document; must be job-related; must be documented |
| Compensation | Pay equity; employment standards on minimum wages |
| Training | Quebec: 1% payroll rule |
| Termination | Employment standards; notice periods; human rights |
