# Job Satisfaction & Organizational Commitment

## Attitudes
A fairly stable evaluative tendency toward an object, consisting of:
1. **Beliefs** — what you perceive to be true
2. **Feelings** — positive or negative evaluations
3. **Behavioural propensity** — tendency to act in certain ways

Formation: Perceived environment + emotion episodes → attitudes → behaviour

---

## Job Satisfaction

### Definition
The degree to which you like/dislike your job.

**Global vs. Facet:**
- *Global:* "How satisfied are you overall with your job?"
- *Facets:* Work itself · Pay · Promotion opportunities · Supervision · Coworkers

### Determinants of Job Satisfaction
Dispositions · Emotions & moods · Job characteristics · Fairness · Goals

### Consequences
- ↑ Job performance (moderate positive relationship)
- ↓ Turnover
- ↓ Absenteeism
- ↑ OCB (Organizational Citizenship Behaviour)
- ↑ Customer satisfaction and profitability

> **Exam tip:** "Happy workers are more productive" is an oversimplification. Relationship is bidirectional and moderate in strength.

### EVLN Model — Responses to Dissatisfaction
| Response | Description | Active/Passive | Constructive/Destructive |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Exit** | Leave (org, dept, supervisor) | Active | Destructive |
| **Voice** | Seek change | Active | Constructive |
| **Loyalty** | Wait for things to improve | Passive | Constructive |
| **Neglect** | Reduce effort; counterproductive behaviour | Passive | Destructive |

## Organizational Commitment

### Three Types
| Type | Definition | Why Stay | Outcomes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Affective** | Emotional attachment and identification with org | *Want to* | ↑ Motivation, OCB, performance, loyalty, creativity |
| **Continuance** | Calculative — too much to lose | *Have to* | ↓ Performance, ↓ OCB, more grievances; stays but disengaged |
| **Normative** | Moral duty and obligation | *Should* | Moderate positive; some cost to creativity |

> **Affective commitment is most desirable.** Continuance commitment is correlated with negative outcomes despite low turnover.

### Building Affective Commitment
| Driver | How |
|---|---|
| **Justice and support** | Organizational justice; caring for employee well-being |
| **Trust** | Management trusting employees first; transparent communication |
| **Shared values** | Value congruence between employee and firm |
| **Employee involvement** | Psychological ownership; social identity |
| **Organizational comprehension** | Clear mental model of the org enables identification |

### Psychological Contracts (from wk10 socialization)
- **Transactional:** Short-term economic; well-defined responsibilities
- **Relational:** Long-term; broad mutual obligations

Normative commitment often develops when relational contracts are perceived and employees feel obligation. Continuance commitment may form when transactional elements are dominant (cost of leaving).
