# Selection

## Definition
The process of choosing individuals who have the relevant qualifications to perform well on a job.

**The core challenge:** Getting information that is *both* reliable and valid.

## Reliability & Validity
- **Reliability:** Consistent results over time
- **Validity:** Measures what it's intended to measure

> A tool can be reliable but not valid (e.g., measures the wrong trait consistently). High reliability ≠ high validity.

## Principles of Good Selection
1. Execution of strategy depends on employee calibre
2. Decisions must reflect job requirements
3. Must integrate with organizational priorities
4. Must recognize organizational constraints
5. Must recognize labour market realities (selection ratio)
6. Must be ethical

## Selection Process (Typical)
Application/resume → Initial HR screening → Tests → Supervisor interview + RJP → Reference check → Hiring decision

## Person-Environment Fit

### Person-Organization Fit (PO Fit)
Compatibility between employee and organizational characteristics (e.g., value congruence).
> Warning: PO fit pursued aggressively → cultural cloning → reduced diversity. See [[concepts/dei]].

### Person-Job Fit (PJ Fit)
- **Demands-abilities (DA):** KSAs match job requirements
- **Needs-supplies (NS):** Employee desires match job content

### Personal Values — Schwartz's Theory
10 values in circumplex structure (opposing values create tension):
Power · Achievement · Hedonism · Stimulation · Self-direction · Universalism · Benevolence · Conformity · Tradition · Security
See [[entities/schwartz-values]]

## Sources of Selection Information

### Application Forms & Resumes
- Application: systematic, quick, minimum requirements
- Resume: additional info on personality, creativity, interests

### Interviews
| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Structured** | Pre-determined questions; ↑ reliability and validity |
| **Unstructured** | Few planned questions; ↓ reliability |
| **Behavioural (BDI)** | Past behaviour predicts future: "Tell me about a time…" |
| **Situational (SJT)** | Hypothetical: "How would you handle…" |
| **Stress** | Rapid questioning to assess stress response |

**Interviewer errors:** Halo effect · Leading questions · Stereotypes · Domination · Contrast errors
**Interviewee errors:** Playing games · Talking too much · Boasting · Unprepared

**To increase validity without reducing reliability:** Align behavioural questions more closely to KSAOs.

### Tests
What you test must be job-related.
| Type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Cognitive ability | Wonderlic, GMA |
| Personality | NEO-PI-R, Big 5 |
| Performance | Dexterity tests |
| Integrity | Stanton survey |
| Substance abuse | Drug tests |

**Big 5 (OCEAN):** Openness · Conscientiousness · Extraversion · Agreeableness · Neuroticism
See [[entities/big5-personality]]

### Reference Checks
Subjective; not highly predictive; should be well-structured.

## Selection Decision
- **Subjective:** Personal judgment
- **Statistical:** Weighted criteria; cut-offs; highest score gets job

## Evaluating Selection
- Yield ratios · Selection ratios · Cost per hire · Time to hire
