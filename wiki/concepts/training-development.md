# Training & Development

## Key Distinctions
| Term | Focus | Timeframe |
|---|---|---|
| **Onboarding** | Adjustment to performance and social aspects | Long-term |
| **Orientation** | Familiarization with org, job, unit | 1–2 days |
| **Socialization** | Internalizing values, norms, beliefs | Ongoing |
| **Training** | KSAOs for *current* work | Task-specific |
| **Development** | KSAOs for *future* work | Career-forward |

**Learning = change in behaviour or knowledge.** All T&D increases human capital.

## The 4-Step Instructional Design Model
1. **Assess needs** (organizational, task, employee levels)
2. **Design & develop the program** (objectives, methods, learning principles)
3. **Implement** (delivery with learning principles in practice)
4. **Evaluate** (Kirkpatrick's 4 levels)

## Needs Analysis — 3 Levels
- **Organizational:** Strategy, environment, resources, culture, constraints
- **Task:** Expected performance — tasks, responsibilities, required KSAOs
- **Employee:** Actual performance — actual KSAOs, barriers to performance

## Principles of Learning
Goal setting · Repetition · Meaningful presentation · Feedback · Individual differences · Active practice · Whole/part; Massed/distributed · Usefulness/Transference

## Learning Theories

**Bandura's Social Learning Theory:**
- Learning through observation of models
- Self-efficacy (belief in one's abilities) determines whether someone imitates a model
- More likely to imitate: similar, knowledgeable, recognized, rewarded, or caring models

**Kolb's Learning Cycle (4 stages):**
1. Concrete Experience → 2. Reflective Observation → 3. Abstract Conceptualization → 4. Active Experimentation → (repeat)
See [[entities/kolb-learning-cycle]]

## Training Methods
| Delivery | Learning Methods |
|---|---|
| On-the-job, apprenticeship, cooperative/internship | Lecture, discussion, case studies |
| Classroom instruction | Role-playing, simulation |
| Self-directed, digital learning | Special projects, management games |
| Virtual/Augmented Reality | Coaching |
| Seminars, job rotation | |

**Choose methods based on:** Cost-effectiveness · Content · Facilities · Trainee preferences · Trainer preferences · Learning principles

## Evaluation: Kirkpatrick's 4 Levels
See [[entities/kirkpatrick-model]]
1. **Reaction** — Did they like it?
2. **Learning** — Did they learn?
3. **Behaviour** — Did they apply it? *(The common gap)*
4. **Results** — Did it improve the business? (ROI)

## Employee Development
Preparing employees for *future* roles/responsibilities. Steps:
1. Assess employees' needs
2. Link competencies to business goals
3. Identify L&D activities
4. Determine resources
5. Identify barriers

**Developmental strategies:**
- *Cognitive:* Lectures, seminars, university (knowledge-focused)
- *Behavioural:* Role-playing, behaviour modelling (behaviour change)
- *Environmental:* Job rotation, temp assignments, internal consulting (context-driven)

## Career Development & RIASEC Model

Organizations with payroll > $2M/year must invest 1% in training or pay a 1% tax. Unique in North America.
