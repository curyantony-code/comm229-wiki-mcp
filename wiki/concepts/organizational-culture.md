# Organizational Culture

## Definition
Collective understanding of beliefs and values that guide how employees act and behave. The "personality of a company." Established by founders, enforced by middle management.

**Espoused vs. Enacted Values:**
- **Espoused:** What the company claims guides its decisions
- **Enacted:** What actually guides decisions and behaviour
> Culture is defined by **enacted** values.

## Artifacts (Observable Signs of Culture)
Because culture is ambiguous and fragmented, multiple artifacts are needed:
1. **Stories & legends** (e.g., founder stories; "can the little person rise?")
2. **Language** (unique terms, phrases)
3. **Rituals** (recurring behaviours, e.g., Free Cone Day)
4. **Ceremonies** (formal events)
5. **Physical structures & symbols** (logos, mottos, office layout)

## Subcultures
- **Dominant culture:** Most widely/consistently shared values
- **Subcultures:** Values in different parts of the organization
- **Countercultures:** Oppose the dominant culture — serve as critical review function and source of emerging values

## Culture Strength

### Three Functions of Strong Cultures
1. **Control system** — behavioural guidance without explicit rules
2. **Social glue** — cohesion and belonging
3. **Sense-making** — interpreting events

### Three Contingencies
1. Culture content must align with the external **environment**
2. Must not become a **cult** (suppresses dissent and critical thinking)
3. Must include an **adaptive culture** (willingness to change)

> Strong culture can suppress creativity, reduce diversity, entrench ethics violations.

### Attraction-Selection-Attrition (ASA) Theory
Culture perpetuates itself through:
- **Attraction:** People self-select into compatible cultures
- **Selection:** Organizations hire compatible people
- **Attrition:** Incompatible people leave or are removed

**Danger:** ASA can reduce cognitive diversity over time.

## Changing Culture
1. Leaders model desired culture (most powerful lever)
2. Align artifacts with desired culture
3. Align rewards and recognition with desired culture
4. Align workforce management with desired culture

## Organizational Socialization
Process by which individuals learn values, expected behaviours, and social knowledge to assume their roles.

**Stages:** Pre-arrival → Encounter → Change & acquisition

**Psychological Contracts:**
- **Transactional:** Short-term economic exchanges; well-defined roles
- **Relational:** Long-term; broad mutual obligations

**Improving Socialization:**
- **RJP:** Balanced positive and negative information — reduces turnover via realistic expectations
- **Socialization agents:** Supervisors (technical info, feedback) and co-workers (norms, social support)
